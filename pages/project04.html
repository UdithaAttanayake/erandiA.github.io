Website Revamp - Intellimorph

A modern, brand new identity for intellimorph

The Beginning

A strong digital presence is key in our highly competitive environment and this story is about a 2-month journey we took as designers to reimagine a website for a fresh, new, updated one. This project was driven by the need to align our online presence, with the changing B2B SaaS market and the limitations found in our current website.

Why Redesign Was Required?

  • Ineffective Communication: • The messaging on the old site failed to effectively communicate our value proposition, often leaving visitors unsure about specific benefits offered by Intellimorph. • Additionally, setting the context and repeating Intellimorph's offerings on each customer contact took a lot of the CEO, Sales, and Marketing teams’ time.
  • User Feedback and Pain Points: • HotJar’s heat maps and user feedback identified a number of pain points, including poor navigation, unclear information architecture, and inaccurate content.
  • Outdated Aesthetics & Functionality: • The old website had an outdated design that failed to reflect the true scope of our offerings. • Functionally, it struggled to deliver an intuitive and efficient user experience.
  • Competitive Landscape: • In a market with fierce competition, it was important that our website stood out as a testament to our innovative thinking and reliability, which can boost brand credibility.

Design Process

#Step 1 — Discover

  • User persona development •User persona development helped us narrow down the primary audience: Leadership, DevOps Engineers, Investors, and potential candidates branched across different pages creating detailed user personas.
  • research and competitive analysis • Thorough research and competitive analysis helped us understand the organization’s mission, vision, and user personas clearly.

#Step 2 — Define

  • Information Architecture: • Information Architecture: We restructured the old site map for a more intuitive and structured navigation experience. Based on this, user flows between sites could be made simpler by eliminating unnecessary pages, merging existing ones, and adding new ones when required.
  • Wireframe: • Wireframe: We sketched low-fidelity wireframes to establish layout, and structure, and validate ideas with our stakeholders. A few screens went through multiple iterations until we found the best fit. Additionally, we developed interactive prototypes for complex screens/flows as and when required.

#Solution Phase

Once logic and functionality are defined, the process of visual design doesn’t take long. The UI was developed using wireframes and the modular system while adhering to the brand’s visual standards. During this stage, well-developed design thinking helps you justify decisions and deal with alternative opinions within the team.

#Step 3 — Develop

  • Content Strategy: While drawing visitors to the website is one part, keeping them engaged on your page is the other challenge. Our objective was to create concise, understandable, and persuasive text because we were aware from the start of how important effective copywriting is.
  • Visual Design: When we had a general idea of the purpose of the website, we needed a system for presenting the vision. We gave Intellimorph a refreshed brand identity, including color schemes, typography, and imagery guidelines

UI Designs

Home Screen- In the new design, we ensured we conveyed what Intellimorph as a product offers at a glance & made it interactive.

Feature Benefits- The old design had too many labels with no relevant categorization along with images being outdated. The new design had every illustration made new in sync with the theme of the website.

Final Outcome

  • Improved User Experience: The redesign significantly enhanced the user experience, resulting in increased engagement.
  • Enhanced Brand Image: Intellimorph’s updated branding and design positioned them as a modern and trustworthy SaaS provider.
  • Clearer Messaging: The refined content and improved messaging clarified Intellimorph’s value proposition, leading them to drive sales better.
  • Competitive Edge:Intellimorph’s revamped website will effectively in the B2B SaaS market.

Conclusion:

  • Having a B2B SaaS cloud platform mindset to a rebranded, updated website required me to have a complete mental shift due to changes in guidelines, user personas, theme, etc. It taught me a lot about people, cross-team collaboration, and how small changes to even smaller interactions matter.
  • Additionally, this gave me an opportunity to let my creative juices flow freely, considering the restrictions that come with B2B SaaS platform designs.
  • It's an amazing and humbling outcome on our goal as a team — To design in a way that positively impacts the business and serves the greater good.
